# Narration critique

Critique of the 68-slide `PR Regression Safety` voice-over. The script is fact-dense and mostly serious, but it often explains the slide rather than adding judgment, contrast, or consequence.

## Ranked findings

### HIGH

- Slides 35-40: The CPU-smoke section is the main attention sag. The listener hears the same claim in five forms: "no G-P-U anywhere", "bounded by disk and pull time, not by capability", "disk, not capability", "no G-P-U anywhere in sight", and "long before any G-P-U compute." Cut one-third of this run. Rewrite as: "The cheap gate catches install and import failures, not runtime CUDA failures. That still covers incidents eight-oh-nine and seven-ninety. The limit is disk: run one production image per matrix leg. Keep the real G-P-U tier for driver, Vulkan, MIG, and training failures."
- Slide 40: Too long for a static proof slide at about 49 seconds by word count, longer with `say` pauses. Offending phrase: "And this is not theory — we built it and ran it this session." It sounds like a demo boast. Rewrite as: "The prototype confirms the boundary. In the Isaac image, the runtime rejected a Python-three-twelve-only dependency before any G-P-U code ran. That is enough to catch the interpreter class of regressions. The evidence is saved with the session."
- Slides 7-11: The primer mostly reads definitions already visible on screen. Offending phrases: "A G-H-S-A is a GitHub Security Advisory", "GitHub Agentic Workflows, or gh-aw, are automation written as a markdown file", and "Good continuous integration is tiered." Add judgment instead of definitions. Example rewrite for slide 10: "The important point is not that checks are tiered; every serious project does that. The failure mode is the required check that goes green without running. This deck treats that as a security problem, not a convenience bug."
- Slides 20, 32, 52: The same blind-CI claim is repeated almost verbatim. Offending phrases: "A green check has repeatedly meant nothing was actually tested", "A green check has repeatedly meant nothing was actually tested", and "Safe-to-merge cannot be asserted." Keep the strongest version on slide 20. Slide 32 should advance the argument: "The next fix is not hardware. First, make the cheap gate prove that dependency graphs still install and import." Slide 52 should be narrower: "The remaining gap is hardware-only: CUDA, drivers, Isaac Vulkan, MIG, and real training."
- Slides 55-56: Slide 56 repeats slide 55 in YAML narration. Offending phrases: "required reviewers", "O-I-D-C — no stored secret", "job sandbox, never on the runner", and "idle cost is essentially zero" appear in both. Cut slide 56 narration to: "The YAML should be read for structure: review approval, environment gate, O-I-D-C login, submit, poll, timeout. The security property is the boundary: secrets stay in GitHub and Azure; contributor code runs in the submitted job."
- Slides 58-61: The Renovate evaluation restates the same comparison too often. Offending phrases: "cross-ecosystem grouping", "not on u-v", "Mend app", "plain GitHub Action", and "scoped spike" recur across four slides. Use one clean progression: slide 58 states the residual problem, slide 59 compares capabilities, slide 60 covers organizational friction, slide 61 gives the decision rule. Cut all repeated "u-v is not the gap" after slide 58.
- Slide 63: The roadmap slide re-argues the whole deck and runs about 50 seconds by word count. Offending phrase: "Here is the whole programme as one phased roadmap." The slide should land, not restart. Rewrite as: "The roadmap separates funding from progress. Phases zero, one, two, and the Renovate spike run on ordinary Actions runners. Phase three waits for funded G-P-U compute. Ship the first four now; do not let the capstone block the regression fixes."

### MED

- Slides 24, 31, 43, 51, 57, 62: Divider slides are too thin: 3-8 seconds except slide 57. Offending phrases: "Configuration-only, shippable in hours", "Now, within days, no Azure", "Also now, no Azure", "This one needs funded compute", and "Bringing it together." Give each divider one useful bridge. Example for slide 43: "The next problem is not detection but disposal: once low-risk dependency changes are green, they should not wait for a maintainer click."
- Slides 2, 13, 21, 27, 37, 46, 52, 54, 55, 63: "Here is" appears ten times and usually starts cold. It weakens the Economist brief. Replace with declarative leads: "The talk has four jobs", "The catalogue splits in two", "The production-image smoke test is simple", "NeMo supplies the precedent", "The G-P-U tier has five controls."
- Slides 25, 32, 44, 52: "The problem" and "The problem today" are stock openers. They are useful once; four times becomes template noise. Replace with the actual problem: "Dependency intake has no risk signal", "CI is blind to the runtime", "Dependabot cannot close the loop", "The G-P-U path is untested."
- Slides 26-30 and 47-50: "And" starts too many sentences and makes transitions sound improvised. Offending phrases: "And on the dependency side", "And concretely", "And the reviewer's cost", "And concretely, auto-merge the trivially safe." Cut the conjunctions. The stronger starts are: "The norms are clear", "Vercel and HuggingFace show the pattern", "Move the reviewer behind CI", "Auto-merge the low-risk patches."
- Slide 6: The Renovate primer is overloaded for a primer. Offending phrase: "a single configuration spanning every ecosystem at once — npm, Python through the pep621 and u-v manager that reads pyproject and the u-v lock, Terraform, Go, and Docker." Rewrite as: "Renovate's edge is breadth: one policy across npm, Python, Terraform, Go, and Docker. Its cost is operational ownership and, for the app, approval."
- Slide 15: The narration lists nearly every runtime stack. Offending phrase: "Reinforcement-learning training on Isaac Lab... Imitation-learning training... Software-in-the-loop evaluation... The data-capture pipeline... A full web application... Four Terraform roots..." The slide can show the list; the voice should interpret it. Rewrite as: "This is not one dependency graph. It is several runtimes sharing a repository but not a failure mode. The same version bump can be harmless in the web app and fatal in Isaac."
- Slide 16: Good point, flabby lead. Offending phrase: "And these are full, production environments — not toy configs." Rewrite as: "These are production contracts. A dependency bump can change the React UI, the FastAPI backend, Terraform providers, or the Isaac training image. The risk is uneven because the runtimes are uneven."
- Slide 23: This reads the three bullets aloud. Offending phrase: "Supply-chain hygiene — batching the low-risk, fast-tracking security, isolating the high-risk." Rewrite as: "The recommendation is not a dependency-bot preference. It is three controls: reduce noisy intake, make CI fail safe, and keep untrusted code away from secrets."
- Slides 45-49: The agentic section repeats the same division of labour. Offending phrases: "read-only", "safe-outputs", "Copilot coding agent", "single updating comment", and "skipping when checks are failing" recur. Keep the safety model on slide 45, the NeMo precedent on slide 46, and the proposed wiring on slide 49. Slide 48 can shrink to: "Separate judgment from execution: gh-aw classifies; a structured issue gives Copilot the repair job."
- Slides 53-56: The security model has good material but too much re-explanation. Offending phrase: "pull request-target" appears as a mini-lesson, then the environment/OIDC/job-sandbox model repeats. Use slide 53 for the threat, slide 55 for this deck's control set, and make slide 56 a short YAML-reading cue.
- Slide 65: The alternatives slide sounds defensive. Offending phrase: "For honesty, the alternatives that were considered and rejected." Rewrite as: "Rejected paths share one flaw: they buy control by adding risk or maintenance. Dependabot replacement adds upkeep; pull-request-target adds secret risk; always-on G-P-U adds cost."
- Slide 68: The close slips into conference politeness. Offending phrase: "Thank you — happy to take questions." For the requested house style, end cleaner: "The finding is simple: runtime-specific regressions are real; most controls need no Azure; the G-P-U gate is designed and waits on funding. I will stop there."

### LOW

- Slides 1-4: The opening spends too much time describing the talk's structure. Offending phrases: "We will start", "Here is the shape of the talk", and "Before the analysis, a short primer." Start with the problem, then orient. Rewrite slide 1 as: "The repo has a dependency-regression problem that green CI does not see. The remedy is not one large platform bet: it is risk-aware intake, fail-safe smoke checks, safe automation, and a funded G-P-U capstone."
- Slides 12, 24, 31, 43, 51, 62: Several transition slides start cold because they name a phase without explaining why it follows. Add one causal link: "Once intake is controlled, the next failure mode is CI blindness"; "Once cheap detection exists, low-risk green PRs can close themselves"; "Once automation is safe, the only remaining assertion is hardware."
- Slides 19, 25: The harmless-patch/CUDA-breaking-major contrast is strong but overused. Offending phrase: "a harmless patch and a CUDA-breaking major look identical" appears in both and has a near duplicate on slide 14. Keep it on slide 14; later say "the intake has no severity or blast-radius signal."
- Slides 29, 50, 63: "fast-tracked by severity" and "low-risk" recur as abstractions. Add one operational verb when repeated: "high advisories bypass cooldown", "patches merge after green CI", "majors produce an issue."
- Slides 33-34: Comparable projects are cited in a rush. Offending phrase: "LeRobot... NVIDIA NeMo... Isaac Lab... PyTorch..." Without a pause, it sounds like name-dropping. Rewrite as two buckets: "The pattern is stable: public forks get cheap tests; scarce G-P-U runs wait for approval. LeRobot, NeMo, Isaac Lab, and PyTorch differ in scale, not in principle."
- Slide 41: The recommendation is a checklist read aloud. Offending phrase: "Check the lock... Install C-P-U torch... Import-smoke... Run the submit scripts... build the evaluation image." Rewrite as: "Make one cheap job prove four things: locks are current, packages import, submission YAML renders, and the evaluation image builds. The torch trick is the only special case."
- Slide 66: The open decisions list is useful, but the voice does not prioritize. Offending phrase: "First... Second... Third... Fourth... And quite separately..." Rewrite as: "Three decisions affect sequencing: branch-protection reality, Dependabot-versus-Renovate order, and the public-fork smoke scope. The torch desync is not a decision; fix it separately."
- Slide 67: High redundancy with the on-slide next-steps bullets. Offending phrase: "File the backlog issue... Pick the first thread... the full research... lives in the tracking folder." Rewrite as: "The first action is administrative: create the backlog issue. The first engineering choice is which no-Azure thread to start. The evidence is already archived."

## Redundancy with slide text

These slides most often read the visible bullets rather than adding interpretation: 5, 7, 8, 10, 11, 20, 23, 25, 32, 33, 44, 52, 55, 64, 65, 66, 67. The worst cases by lexical overlap are slides 67, 20, 44, 10, 11, 8, 7, 52, 23, and 5. The fix is not shorter narration everywhere; it is different narration: claim what the slide means, not what the slide says.

## Length balance and listenability

No slide exceeds 60 seconds at 150 words per minute, but the default `say` voice and code-heavy pauses will push the longest slides close to a static-slide minute. The worst offenders are slides 59, 60, and 63 at about 50-53 seconds by word count; slide 40 at 49 seconds; slides 6, 11, and 36 at 46-47 seconds; slides 35 and 37 at 42-44 seconds. The bigger risk is clustering: slides 35-40 and 58-63 create long runs of dense exposition with little tonal change. Shorten those clusters before trimming isolated slides.

## Stock phrases to purge

- "Here is" appears on slides 2, 13, 21, 27, 37, 46, 52, 54, 55, and 63. Purge most of them.
- "The problem" or "The problem today" appears on slides 1, 2, 25, 32, 44, and 52. Use the specific problem instead.
- "And concretely" or "Concretely" appears on slides 27, 37, 49, and 50. It sounds like presenter scaffolding.
- "Today" appears often enough to become a crutch, especially across slides 13-30 and 39-49. Keep it only for before/after contrasts.
- "The recommendation" appears on slides 28, 41, and 61. Prefer the recommended action as the subject.
- "The key" appears on slides 4, 5, 15, and 63. Name the key point directly.
- "no G-P-U anywhere", "disk, not capability", "bounded by disk", "fast-tracked by severity", and "green check" are strong once; repeated, they become slogans.

## Single biggest narration improvement

Use a strict rule per slide: say one new claim, one piece of evidence not already legible on the slide, and one implication. Cut every sentence that paraphrases the bullet text or re-proves an earlier claim.
