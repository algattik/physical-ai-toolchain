How to use

Goal

Reset a physical scene to match the starting frame of a recorded episode, so the next demonstration begins from the same pose.

Workflow

  1. Pick a camera in the header — the live feed appears immediately.
  2. Click 📁 Choose dataset, hover cards to preview, click one.
  3. In the episode grid, filter by SUCCESS/FAILURE tags or task name, hover an episode to see its motion, click to load its first frame as the reference.
  4. Adjust props in the scene until the live feed cancels the inverted reference to flat gray. The Alignment gauge (top-right) climbs as you improve.

Controls

  • Reference / Live opacity — linear blend weights. With one layer inverted at 50/50, matched pixels collapse to flat mid-gray.
  • invert — flip a layer's pixel values. The standard recipe inverts exactly one of the two layers; inverting both undoes the cancellation (matched pixels show as the inverted scene instead of gray).
  • Hover the composite for a magnifying-glass loupe at the cursor.
  • ↺ on the Alignment panel resets the session-best baseline.

Reading the Alignment gauge

  • Lighting-invariant gradient cross-correlation. Higher = better.
  • Auto-zooms to the last ~12 s window — small adjustments stay visible.
  • ▲ +0.27 means you just beat your session best; ▼ -0.18 means you regressed.
  • "low texture" appears when the scene has too few edges to score reliably.

Episode tags

Tags are read live from meta/episode_labels.json in each dataset (same format the dataviewer writes). Edits there show up here without restart.
